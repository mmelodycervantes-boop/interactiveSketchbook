Reflection

This week, I decided to focus on designing the interface prototype for my micro project (Terms and Conditions) and Sketch 9 (which includes the images of Blueberry). For both, I decided to keep the same layout as the home page, which I designed last week, to keep the consistency within the design. I also used the different headings to show hierarchy. The title “Mels interactive Sketchbooks” is the highest, followed by the “home,” and “menu,” then the sketch or project name, then the sub-heading, and finally the body. Doing this, it helps to create a natural flow from most important to the least important.

From my previous version, I feel like nothing really changed concerning both of the designs themselves, since I couldn’t figure out how to make Figma interactive, or if it even is interactive. If I were to have figured out how it was interactive, I would have added instant feedback if the user did something wrong during the Terms and Conditions micro-project. However, I did decide to keep the scroll bar and dots to show the user where they are in the system. I also added a back and forward button so that users can return to where they previously were or to move forward with the sketches/project.

The problem that my audit addresses is the consistency. All the pages look the same due to the consistent layout. The pages also use the same visual patterns. In doing so, the users don’t have to figure things out on every page, but rather already know due to interacting with the interface in the first place. Before, all my pages looked different with no clear way to interact with the system, but now it seems more whole.

In my sketchbook entries I have provided my ideation process and my final version of the project. In my “microIdeas,” I have depicted three ideas of what my project will take focus on. One of them being the “terms and conditions” idea. The other ideas were “Priority” which takes on a fast-pass in a long line idea and “Get Heard” which focuses on communication. In my “TermsAndConditions_Ideation,” I sketch out the process in which became the foundation of how the system would operate. It depicts the consequences of the users actions, points of restrictions, and changes in the system. In “TermsAndConditions_Final,” the final version of the system is shown. However, some changes were made due the complication of the code. Instead of text being added the faster one scrolls, it was replaced with a penalty of a warning box, followed by a 5 second timeout. This made the system much cleaner in feel. I also did not make a separate copy of my progress check’s, but rather update it within the same file, so there is no early forms of the development process.

After these prototypes, I wonder if the Figma interface can be interactive? Right now, I feel like I can’t really fully figure out how the user will interact with the interface. Such as how the buttons will work, where they might get confused, etc. To me, that's what feels unresolved, not knowing exactly how the user will interact with the system on a mobile interface.
