Interface Prototype - Week 1

Prototype - Landing Page

I decided to focus on iterating my landing page for my micro-project. Some of the structural decisions I made this week involved creating a clear hierarchy of information. To do this, I used different font sizes for the heading, subheading, and body text. I also placed the different text elements within specific areas to make the hierarchy more clear. For example, the title is at the very top, and the subheadings each have their own space. The hierarchy of information is further clarified by organizing content into categories. I created four categories: one for how to explore the interactive sketchbook, the second for the exploratory sketches and their links, the third for my Terms and Conditions micro-project, and the fourth for my reflections and audits.

What changed from my previous version is that there is no longer a single row of links. Instead, the links are categorized into different sections so that users can more clearly understand their purpose. I also added a way for users to navigate the site more easily. If they wish to return to the home page, they can click “HOME,” or, on mobile, they can use the forward and back arrows. I also included instructions explaining how users can navigate through the interactive sketchbook.

From my audit, these revisions address consistency, as the interface now feels more familiar compared to other common web designs. I used triangles for dropdown menus, arrows to move forward and back, and a home button. The information is also organized into sections, which makes it easier for users to understand what the links are for.

What still feels unresolved is how users can navigate between pages on a computer, since I did not include forward or back arrows in the desktop interface.
